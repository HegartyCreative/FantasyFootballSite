2016 Review


2016

Cup
Julian Morris

The 17th season of the Fantasy League has just been completed and Sean’s Teletext Town wins the title for the second time in 3 years. Andy’s Bray Head were the runners-up, his best ever Fantasy finish, and Julian’s Hophead are the Cup winners. We offer our hearty congratulations to all.

Now let’s take a look at the season’s highlights.

The same 16 Managers as last season did battle for this year’s honours. However only 14 could be in attendance for the Auction meeting as Steve B (Cardiff for the Rugby World Cup) and Glen (work commitments in Hull) were unable to join us. The Auction meeting was held for the 9th year running in the upstairs private room at The Calthorpe Arms, Grays Inn Road.

The top buys at the Auction were: £24 million S. Aguero (Man City) Ian Henry, £19 million W. Rooney (Man Utd) Steve Blantz and £18 million D. Sturridge (Liv) by Andy Luck.

The first week of the season saw, reigning champions, Gateaux lose 4-2 to Teletext Town. Puma Kings thrashed Spooners 6-0, Kick up the R’s beat Bray Head 2-1 and Gaucho beat Chedi Warriors 3-0.

The second week saw Spooners hammered again this time 6-3 by Gaucho, Loughton Knights win 4-1 against Kick up the R’s, Verry Tenables win 2-1 against Invisible Magyars and Silent Bidders beat Bray Head 1-0. Top of the league were Gaucho with 6pts, Loughton Knights 6pts, and Teletext Town with 6pts. At the bottom of the league sat Spooners, Invisible Magyars and Bray Head all on zero points.

Week 4 saw Kick up the R’s beat Teletext Town 1-0, Clowns Pocket beat Puma Kings 3-0, Shades of Grays win 2-0 against Gaucho and Bray Head picked up their first win with a 2-1 victory over Verry Tenables.

Week 6 saw Spooners win 2-1 against Gateaux and Dynamo Redhill win 1-0 over Shades of Grays. Invisible Magyars was the only team yet to pick up a point. The league standings were: (1) Loughton Knights 15pts, (2) Kick up the R’s 15pts, (3) Silent Bidders 13pts and (4) Teletext Town 11 pts.

Week 8 results saw The Magyars win their first game a 2-0 victory against Gaucho, Hophead beat Verry Tenables 3-1, Teletext Town drew 0-0 with Spooners and Gateaux and Chedi Warriors ended 1-1. The table now looked like this (1) Loughton Knights 19pts, (2) Silent Bidders 17pts, (3) Kick up the R’s 17pts and (4) Teletext Town 15 pts. Invisible Magyars were still at the foot of the table with 4pts.

Weeks 9 and 10 were the first 2 games in the group Cup games. Gaucho beat Bray Head 4-1, Puma Kings beat Invisible Magyars 3-2, Gateaux won 1-0 against Kick up the R’s, Hophead won 2-0 against Loughton Knights, Shades of Grays beat Gateaux 2-1, Silent Bidders won 3-1 against Chedi Warriors and Verry Tenables won 1-0 against Teletext Town.

Week 12 saw Chedi Warriors beat Kick up the R’s 3-2. Clowns Pocket won 3-2 against Shades of Grays and Teletext Town beat Hophead 1-0. Top of the table looked like this: Teletext Town 21pts, Loughton Knights 20pts, Kick up the R’s 20pts, Silent Bidders 19pts, and Bray Head 17pts.

Week 13 was the final Cup group matches. Kick up the R’s won 3-2 against Shades of Grays, Silent Bidders won 2-0 against Teletext Town, Hophead drew 0-0 with Spooners, Puma Kings drew 0-0 with Bray Head, and Loughton Knights also drew 0-0 against Dynamo Redhill. The group winners were Puma Kings, Kick up the R’s, Hophead and Silent Bidders, the runners up were Gaucho, Shades of Grays, Loughton Knights and Verry Tenables.

Week 14 saw the Cup quarter finals, and the winners were Puma Kings 3-1 against Shades of Grays after a replay. Loughton Knights 3-2 over Kick up the R’s after a replay. Hophead 1-0 against Verry Tenables and Silent Bidders 1-0 against Gaucho after a replay.

Week 15 saw the cup semi-finals take place. Silent Bidders beat Puma Kings 2-0 to go through and Hophead beat Loughton Knights 1-0 in a replay after a 0-0 draw.

Week 17 saw Teletext Town win 1-0 against Chedi Warriors. Bray Head won 2-0 against Invisible Magyars. Shades of Grays beat Gateaux 3-1 and Silent Bidders won 2-1 against Verry Tenables. The table looked like this (1) Teletext Town 27 pts, (2) Bray Head 23pts, (3) Silent Bidders 22pts, (4) Loughton Knights 21pts.

Weeks 18 and 19 were the first and second legs of The Cup Final the first leg saw Hophead beat Silent Bidders 1-0, and he repeated this feat in the 2nd leg also to take the cup on a 2-0 aggregate. This was Julian first Fantasy Cup win.

Week 20 saw Teletext Town beat Dynamo Redhill 4-2. Bray Head won 1-0 against Clowns Pocket and Silent Bidders beat Hophead 2-1 to gain some revenge for their defeat in the cup final the week before. The following week (week 21) saw the penultimate league games, Bray Head beat the table toppers Teletext Town 1-0 to open up the title race. (1) Teletext Town 30 pts, (2) Bray Head 29pts, (3) Silent Bidders 26 pts, (4) Kick up the R’s 25pts, Invisible Magyars were propping up the league on 8pts.

The final league games saw Teletext Town draw 1-1 with Silent Bidders, but Bray Head also drew 0-0 with Shades of Grays so Teletext Town were the champions and Bray Head finished as runners up. Kick up the R’s won 2-1 against Dynamo Redhill to grab 3rd place. There were also final day wins for Gaucho 3-0 over Gateaux, Puma Kings 2-0 against Loughton Knights, Clowns Pocket 2-1 over the Magyars, Hophead 3-0 against Chedi Warriors and Verry Tenables 3-2 over Spooners.


Star team status was achieved by twelve teams during the campaign. However the teams that gained the accolade more than once were: Silent Bidders 3, Gaucho 3, Teletext Town 2, Bray Head 2, Hophead 2, Loughton Knights 2 and Shades of Grays 2.


  • The transfer windows had an average of 9 managers involved
  • Sean Hegarty has won the league for the second time, the last time was 2013 he also equalled his best ever points total of 31pts
  • Julian Morris joins the band of 7 previous managers that have won both the league and the cup
  • Andy Cummins attained his best ever finishing position, runners-up and most points in a season 30pts
  • Paul Lambertini had his best ever finishing position (10th), and his highest points haul of 18pts
  • Finally congratulations to Sean, Andy and Julian on their achievements this season. Who knows? Next season it could be you!
